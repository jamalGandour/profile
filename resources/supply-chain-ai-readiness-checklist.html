Supply Chain & AI Readiness Checklist

Use this checklist to identify practical improvement opportunities in inventory, demand planning, dashboards, reporting automation, AI tools, customer delivery follow-up, and team capability before starting a project.

Step 1

Quick Opportunity Scan

Inventory & Stock Health

  • Do you track slow-moving, obsolete, shortage, and expiry risk weekly?
  • Do you have ABC classification and replenishment priorities?
  • Can managers see stock risk without waiting for manual reports?

Demand Planning

  • Do planners review forecast exceptions with sales and operations?
  • Are assumptions, promotions, and customer changes documented?
  • Do you measure forecast bias and forecast accuracy by product group?

KPI & Reporting Control

  • Are your KPIs connected to service, inventory, cost, and productivity?
  • Are reports automated enough to support fast weekly decisions?
  • Do executives receive clear root-cause notes, not only numbers?

AI & Automation Readiness

  • Have you selected practical AI use cases linked to business value?
  • Do teams have prompt templates, rules, and governance?
  • Can AI support analysis, reporting, customer follow-up, or inventory alerts?

Step 2

Score Your Readiness

Score Meaning Recommended Action
0-8 High opportunity and low visibility. Start with a supply chain diagnosis and data cleanup roadmap.
9-16 Some structure exists, but control is inconsistent. Build KPI dashboards, planning routines, and selected automation.
17-24 Good foundation for AI-enabled improvement. Pilot AI agents, automated reporting, and decision-support workflows.

Step 3

Recommended 30 / 60 / 90 Day Plan

First 30 Days

  • Diagnose current supply chain pain points and data quality.
  • Select priority KPIs and quick-win reports.
  • Define AI use cases with clear business value.

Days 31-60

  • Build dashboard, templates, and decision routines.
  • Train teams on practical AI and analysis workflows.
  • Launch pilot automation or AI assistant.

Days 61-90

  • Measure impact on inventory, service, reporting speed, and control.
  • Improve SOPs and management cadence.
  • Scale successful use cases to more teams or categories.
